## MAJOR EUROPEAN POWERS (non-HRE)

### GREAT BRITAIN
**Territorial extent**: England, Wales, Scotland, Ireland (entire island under British control post-1691); Hanover in personal union (separate entry below).
**Capital**: London
**Key cities**: Edinburgh, Dublin, Bristol, Liverpool, Manchester, Birmingham
**Justification**: Acts of Union 1707 united England-Wales with Scotland; Ireland under English/British rule since Williamite War (1689-91).
**Source**: Black, Jeremy. *Eighteenth-Century Britain, 1688-1783* (2001).

### FRANCE (Bourbon)
**Territorial extent**: Roughly modern France minus Corsica (Genoese until 1768), minus Lorraine (French since 1766), minus Savoy/Nice (Savoyard). Includes Alsace (acquired 1648-81), Franche-Comté.
**Capital**: Versailles/Paris
**Key cities**: Lyon, Marseille, Bordeaux, Rouen, Strasbourg, Lille
**Justification**: Louis XV; territorial losses in Seven Years' War were colonial (Canada, India), not European.
**Source**: Doyle, William. *The Oxford History of the French Revolution* (1989), ch. 1.

### SPAIN (Bourbon)
**Territorial extent**: Iberian peninsula minus Portugal; Balearic Islands; Spanish Netherlands LOST (to Austria 1714); Naples & Sicily under a Bourbon cadet (separate entry).
**Capital**: Madrid
**Key cities**: Barcelona, Seville, Valencia, Cádiz, Zaragoza
**Justification**: Charles III (r. 1759-88); War of Spanish Succession settled Spanish Bourbon legitimacy but cost European possessions.
**Source**: Lynch, John. *Bourbon Spain, 1700-1808* (1989).

### PORTUGAL
**Territorial extent**: Modern Portugal, including Algarve.
**Capital**: Lisbon
**Key cities**: Porto, Coimbra, Évora
**Justification**: Independent kingdom since 1640 (end of Iberian Union); under José I (1750-77), effective rule by Pombal.
**Source**: Maxwell, Kenneth. *Pombal: Paradox of the Enlightenment* (1995).

### RUSSIA (Romanov)
**Territorial extent**: European Russia to the Urals; Baltic provinces (Livonia, Estonia, Ingria acquired 1721); Finland PARTLY (Vyborg/Karelia only; rest is Swedish until 1809); Ukraine (Left Bank + Sloboda + Zaporizhian Sich); Crimea NOT YET (independent Khanate; annexed 1783).
**Capital**: St. Petersburg
**Key cities**: Moscow, Kiev, Riga, Smolensk, Kazan, Astrakhan
**Justification**: Catherine II (acceded 1762); post-Great Northern War (1721) Russia is a major European power; Crimean Khanate still independent, Ottoman vassal.
**Source**: Dixon, Simon. *Catherine the Great* (2009); Fisher, Alan. *The Crimean Tatars* (1978), ch. 3.
**ANACHRONISM FLAG**: Crimea must be independent (Crimean Khanate, Ottoman vassal) in 1763.

### OTTOMAN EMPIRE
**Territorial extent in Europe**: Balkans (modern Bulgaria, North Macedonia, most of Serbia, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Albania, Kosovo, parts of Romania—Wallachia & Moldavia as vassal principalities); Greece (entire mainland + islands); Crimean Khanate as vassal (Crimea itself).
**Capital**: Constantinople/Istanbul
**Key cities in Europe**: Edirne (Adrianople), Thessaloniki (Salonica), Sofia, Belgrade, Sarajevo, Athens
**Justification**: Mustafa III (1757-74); Ottoman control of Balkans intact until Russo-Turkish War (1768-74) and subsequent erosion.
**Source**: Finkel, Caroline. *Osman's Dream: The History of the Ottoman Empire* (2005), ch. 16.
**ANACHRONISM FLAG**: Wallachia & Moldavia are vassal principalities, not fully sovereign. Crimea is an Ottoman-vassal Khanate (independent ruler, Giray dynasty).

### POLAND-LITHUANIA (Commonwealth)
**Territorial extent**: ENTIRE pre-partition territory — modern Poland, Lithuania, Belarus, much of Ukraine (Right Bank), Latvia, parts of western Russia (Smolensk lost to Russia 1667, but rest intact).
**Capital**: Warsaw (administrative); Kraków (coronation city)
**Key cities**: Vilnius, Gdańsk (Danzig), Lviv (Lwów), Poznań, Lublin
**Justification**: Augustus III (r. 1733-63; died Oct 1763); First Partition is 1772 — in Feb 1763 Poland is WHOLE.
**Source**: Lukowski, Jerzy & Zawadzki, Hubert. *A Concise History of Poland* (2nd ed., 2006), ch. 4.
**ANACHRONISM FLAG**: Poland must be shown at full pre-partition extent. Any partition of Poland before 1772 is anachronistic.

### DENMARK-NORWAY
**Territorial extent**: Denmark proper (Jutland peninsula, Funen, Zealand, Bornholm); Norway (entire); duchies of Schleswig & Holstein (in personal union; complex status within HRE for Holstein).
**Capital**: Copenhagen
**Key cities**: Bergen, Christiania (Oslo), Trondheim, Odense, Aarhus
**Justification**: Frederick V (r. 1746-66); the twin kingdoms united since 1524 (Norway subordinate).
**Source**: Derry, T.K. *A History of Scandinavia* (1979), ch. 6.

### SWEDEN
**Territorial extent**: Sweden proper; Finland (entire; lost to Russia 1809); Swedish Pomerania (around Stralsund; last remnant of Swedish Empire).
**Capital**: Stockholm
**Key cities**: Gothenburg, Uppsala, Turku (Åbo), Viborg (ceded to Russia 1721, so NOT Swedish in 1763).
**Justification**: Adolf Frederick (r. 1751-71); Age of Liberty (parliamentary supremacy 1719-72); Sweden much reduced from its 17th-century empire but retains Finland.
**Source**: Roberts, Michael. *The Age of Liberty: Sweden 1719-1772* (1986).
**CORRECTION**: Vyborg/Karelia ceded to Russia 1721 (Treaty of Nystad).

### DUTCH REPUBLIC (United Provinces)
**Territorial extent**: Seven provinces (Holland, Zeeland, Utrecht, Gelderland, Overijssel, Friesland, Groningen) + Generality Lands (Brabant, Limburg, Flanders areas).
**Capital**: The Hague (seat of government); Amsterdam (largest city)
**Key cities**: Rotterdam, Utrecht, Leiden, Haarlem, Groningen
**Justification**: William V (Stadtholder from 1751, but a minor until 1766; regency period). Republic in decline post-1713 but still independent.
**Source**: Israel, Jonathan. *The Dutch Republic: Its Rise, Greatness, and Fall, 1477-1806* (1995), Part V.

### SWISS CONFEDERATION
**Territorial extent**: Thirteen cantons (core) + associated territories; roughly modern Switzerland minus Geneva (independent city-republic, not yet Swiss) & Neuchâtel (Prussian possession 1707-1848).
**Capital**: None (confederation); Bern, Zürich, Luzern as major centers
**Key cities**: Basel, Geneva (independent), Lausanne
**Justification**: Confederation of sovereign cantons; neutrality established 1648.
**Source**: Bonjour, Edgar et al. *A Short History of Switzerland* (1952), ch. 8.

## HOLY ROMAN EMPIRE — ABSTRACTION TIERS

**Goal**: Model ~15-30 distinct HRE tags for plausibility without drowning in microstates.

### TIER 1: MUST KEEP (Major Territorial Powers — 20 states)

#### 1. AUSTRIA (Habsburg Hereditary Lands)
**Extent**: Archduchy of Austria, Styria, Carinthia, Carniola, Tyrol, Vorarlberg, Bohemia (kingdom), Moravia, Silesia (LOST to Prussia except Austrian Silesia, a sliver), Austrian Netherlands (Belgium), Milan (Lombardy), Tuscany (Habsburg-Lorraine from 1737).
**Capital**: Vienna
**Ruler**: Maria Theresa (1740-80)
**Source**: Ingrao, Charles. *The Habsburg Monarchy, 1618-1815* (2nd ed., 2000).
**Note**: Silesia lost to Prussia 1742-48, confirmed 1763.

#### 2. BRANDENBURG-PRUSSIA (Kingdom of Prussia)
**Extent**: Brandenburg (electorate), Prussia (kingdom, outside HRE), Pomerania (Hither & Further), Magdeburg, Halberstadt, Minden, Ravensberg, Cleves, Mark, East Frisia (from 1744), Silesia (conquered 1742-45).
**Capital**: Berlin (& Potsdam)
**Ruler**: Frederick II "the Great" (1740-86)
**Source**: Clark, Christopher. *Iron Kingdom: The Rise and Downfall of Prussia, 1600-1947* (2006), ch. 5.

#### 3. SAXONY (Electorate)
**Extent**: Electoral Saxony (Dresden, Leipzig, Meissen).
**Capital**: Dresden
**Ruler**: Frederick Christian (1763, died Dec 1763); Augustus III (d. Oct 1763) was also King of Poland.
**Source**: Blanning, T.C.W. *The Culture of Power and the Power of Culture* (2002), ch. 6.

#### 4. BAVARIA (Electorate)
**Extent**: Duchy/Electorate of Bavaria, Upper Palatinate (gained 1628).
**Capital**: Munich
**Ruler**: Maximilian III Joseph (1745-77)
**Source**: Vann, James Allen & Rowan, Steven W. *The Old Reich: Essays on German Political Institutions 1495-1806* (1974).

#### 5. HANOVER (Electorate of Brunswick-Lüneburg)
**Extent**: Principality of Calenberg, Göttingen, Grubenhagen, Lüneburg (electoral dignity 1692).
**Capital**: Hanover
**Ruler**: George III (also King of Great Britain, 1760-1820; personal union)
**Source**: Hatton, Ragnhild. *George I: Elector and King* (1978).
**Note**: Hanover in personal union with Britain but distinct HRE entity.

#### 6. WÜRTTEMBERG (Duchy)
**Extent**: Swabian duchy centered on Stuttgart.
**Capital**: Stuttgart
**Ruler**: Charles Eugene (1737-93)
**Source**: Wilson, Peter H. *The Holy Roman Empire: A Thousand Years of Europe's History* (2016), ch. 5.

#### 7. PALATINATE (Electorate / Kurpfalz)
**Extent**: Electoral Palatinate (Heidelberg, Mannheim), Rhine Palatinate. Upper Palatinate transferred to Bavaria 1628.
**Capital**: Mannheim (from 1720s)
**Ruler**: Charles IV Theodore (1742-99, later also Bavaria 1777)
**Source**: Hughes, Michael. *Early Modern Germany, 1477-1806* (1992), ch. 5.

#### 8. HESSE-KASSEL (Landgraviate)
**Extent**: Northern Hesse, centered on Kassel.
**Capital**: Kassel
**Ruler**: Frederick II (1760-85)
**Source**: Ingrao, *The Hessian Mercenary State* (1987).

#### 9. HESSE-DARMSTADT (Landgraviate)
**Extent**: Southern Hesse, centered on Darmstadt.
**Capital**: Darmstadt
**Ruler**: Louis IX (1768-90); in 1763, Louis VIII (1739-68)
**Source**: (Same as Hesse-Kassel)

#### 10. MAINZ (Electorate & Archbishopric)
**Extent**: Ecclesiastical electorate, territories along middle Rhine & Main.
**Capital**: Mainz
**Ruler**: Prince-Archbishop Emmerich Joseph von Breidbach (1763-74)
**Source**: Wilson, *Holy Roman Empire*, ch. 3.

#### 11. COLOGNE (Electorate & Archbishopric)
**Extent**: Ecclesiastical electorate, territories in Lower Rhine & Westphalia (Bonn, etc.).
**Capital**: Bonn (residence; city of Cologne itself was a free city)
**Ruler**: Prince-Archbishop Maximilian Frederick (1761-84)
**Source**: (Same)

#### 12. TRIER (Electorate & Archbishopric)
**Extent**: Ecclesiastical electorate, territories along Moselle.
**Capital**: Trier
**Ruler**: Prince-Archbishop Johann IX Philipp von Walderdorff (1756-68)
**Source**: (Same)

#### 13. MÜNSTER (Prince-Bishopric)
**Extent**: Ecclesiastical principality in Westphalia.
**Capital**: Münster
**Ruler**: Prince-Bishop Maximilian Frederick (same person as Cologne; personal union common for ecclesiastical states)
**Source**: (Same)

#### 14. BADEN (Margraviate, split into Baden-Durlach & Baden-Baden)
**Extent**: Southwestern territories; by 1763 functionally two states: Baden-Durlach (Protestant, Karlsruhe) & Baden-Baden (Catholic, Baden-Baden); reunified 1771.
**Capitals**: Karlsruhe (Baden-Durlach), Baden-Baden (Baden-Baden)
**Rulers**: Charles Frederick of Baden-Durlach (1738-1811); Augustus George of Baden-Baden (1761-71)
**Source**: Vann & Rowan, *The Old Reich*.
**Note**: Reasonable to model as one tag or two; Baden-Durlach is larger and more significant.

#### 15. MECKLENBURG (Duchies: Mecklenburg-Schwerin & Mecklenburg-Strelitz)
**Extent**: Northern duchies on Baltic coast.
**Capitals**: Schwerin (Mecklenburg-Schwerin), Neustrelitz (Mecklenburg-Strelitz)
**Rulers**: Frederick II (Schwerin, 1756-85); Adolphus Frederick IV (Strelitz, 1752-94)
**Source**: (Same)
**Note**: Two duchies, but often treated as one "Mecklenburg" in grand-strategy abstraction.

#### 16. HOLSTEIN (Duchy, complex ownership)
**Extent**: Southern Schleswig-Holstein region; partly Danish-controlled, partly Imperial (independent Gottorp line until 1773).
**Capital**: Kiel (Holstein-Gottorp)
**Ruler**: Complex — Danish king holds some; Russian tsar (Peter III, then Paul I) held Holstein-Gottorp claim until 1773.
**Source**: Derry, *A History of Scandinavia*, ch. 6.
**Note**: 1763 is pre-1773 settlement; can abstract as Danish or separate.

#### 17. BRUNSWICK-WOLFENBÜTTEL (Duchy)
**Extent**: Duchy in Lower Saxony, separate from Hanover branch.
**Capital**: Wolfenbüttel (later Brunswick)
**Ruler**: Charles I (1735-80)
**Source**: Wilson, *Holy Roman Empire*.

#### 18. SALZBURG (Prince-Archbishopric)
**Extent**: Ecclesiastical principality in Alps.
**Capital**: Salzburg
**Ruler**: Prince-Archbishop Sigismund III (1753-71)
**Source**: (Same)

#### 19. WÜRZBURG (Prince-Bishopric)
**Extent**: Ecclesiastical principality in Franconia.
**Capital**: Würzburg
**Ruler**: Prince-Bishop Adam Friedrich von Seinsheim (1755-79)
**Source**: (Same)

#### 20. BAMBERG (Prince-Bishopric)
**Extent**: Ecclesiastical principality in Franconia, often in personal union with Würzburg.
**Capital**: Bamberg
**Ruler**: (Same as Würzburg in 1763)
**Source**: (Same)

### TIER 4: ABSTRACT AWAY (everything smaller)

- Hundreds of Imperial Knights' territories (Reichsritterschaft)
- Tiny counts (e.g., Isenburg, Wied, Solms, Leiningen, Hohenlohe, etc.)
- Tiny prince-abbeys (e.g., Fulda, Kempten, Corvey, Quedlinburg, etc.) — EXCEPT **Fulda** (Prince-Abbacy) is large enough to consider keeping.
- Tiny free cities (e.g., Rothenburg, Worms, Speyer, Ulm, Regensburg, etc.)

**Abstraction method**: Fold into nearest major neighbor:
- Thuringian micro-duchies (Saxe-Weimar, Saxe-Gotha, Saxe-Meiningen, etc.) → aggregate as "Ernestine Saxony" or fold into Electoral Saxony.
- Swabian & Franconian micro-counts → fold into Württemberg, Bavaria, Ansbach/Bayreuth.
- Westphalian micro-states → fold into Münster, Cologne, Hesse-Kassel, or Prussia.
- Rhenish micro-states → fold into Mainz, Trier, Palatinate.
