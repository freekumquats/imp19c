# North America 1763 — Ground-Truth Territorial Control Audit

Coverage: modern USA, Canada, Arctic, Alaska (north of the Rio Grande). February 1763,
following the Treaty of Paris ending the Seven Years' War / French and Indian War.

**Key context**
- **Treaty of Paris (Feb 10, 1763):** France cedes Canada + all land east of the Mississippi to Britain; Louisiana west of the Mississippi → Spain.
- **Royal Proclamation (Oct 7, 1763):** Britain reserves the trans-Appalachian interior as "Indian Territory."
- **Pontiac's War (1763–66):** multi-tribal resistance to British occupation of former French forts.
- **Russian expansion:** promyshlenniki probing the Aleutians from the 1760s; no permanent settlement until 1784 (Kodiak).
- **Indigenous majority:** the interior west of the Mississippi, the Plains, the Pacific NW, the Arctic, and most of the continent by area remained under Indigenous control.

---

## I. European colonial holdings

### GBR — British America
Atlantic seaboard 13 colonies; Quebec/Canada (newly ceded 1763); Newfoundland; Nova Scotia; Hudson Bay Company posts (Rupert's Land, de facto coastal only). British sovereignty asserted over ceded territories, trans-Appalachian west legally reserved to Native nations. (Anderson, *Crucible of War* 2000; Calloway, *The Scratch of a Pen* 2006.)
- HBC (Hudson's Bay Company) — Rupert's Land, royal charter 1670; de facto = a string of coastal/river posts (York Factory capital; Fort Churchill, Moose Factory, Fort Albany). Distinct trade-company tag or GBR subject.

### SPA / NSP — Spain / New Spain northern frontier
- **Louisiana** (from France, Fontainebleau 1762; Spanish admin arrives 1766) — nominal control of lower Mississippi + posts (New Orleans, St. Louis founded 1764).
- **Texas** (San Antonio de Béxar), **New Mexico** (Santa Fe, upper Rio Grande Pueblos), **Baja California** (Loreto).
- **Alta California NOT yet colonized** — first missions 1769 (San Diego)/1770 (Monterey). Should NOT be a controlled territory in 1763.
- **Spanish Florida CEDED to Britain 1763** (exchange for Havana); Britain splits into East/West Florida. Spain regains 1783.
(Weber, *The Spanish Frontier in North America* 1992; Hackel, *Children of Coyote* 2005.)

### Russian America — NASCENT, not a held territory in 1763
Aleutian probes only; region Indigenous-controlled (Aleut/Unangan, Alutiiq, Tlingit, Haida, Athabaskan). (Vinkovetsky 2011; Black 2004.)

## II. Indigenous polities — TAG vs CULTURE-ONLY

Criterion: TAG = territorial control + political organization (confederacy/council) + treaty/military capacity. Stateless/band-level = culture-only.

### Eastern Woodlands & Great Lakes (existing tags, ✅ = mod already has)
- **IRO Haudenosaunee** ✅ — upstate NY, Ontario; Grand Council at Onondaga.
- **CHE Cherokee** ✅ — S. Appalachians; capital Chota.
- **CRK Creek/Muscogee** ✅ — GA/AL; Coweta + Tuckabatchee.
- **CHO Choctaw** ✅ — MS/LA; Nanih Waiya.
- **CHI Chickasaw** ✅ — N. MS/W. TN. (NOTE: tag collision — CHI is the Qing player tag in this mod; Chickasaw must use a different code.)
- **CTF Council of Three Fires** ✅ — Great Lakes (Ojibwe/Odawa/Potawatomi); Michilimackinac.
- **SHW Shawnee** ✅ — Ohio Valley.
- **MIA Miami** ✅ — Indiana/Ohio; Kekionga.
- **ILL Illinois** ✅ — Illinois R. valley (declining/minor).

- **LAK Lakota** ✅ — emerging N. Plains power (Black Hills), still consolidating in 1763.
- **DAK Dakota** ✅ — MN, E. SD.
- **BLA Blackfoot** ✅ — S. Alberta/N. Montana (Bow River).
- **PAW Pawnee** ✅ — Nebraska (Platte/Loup).

### Great Basin & Plateau

- **UTE Ute** ✅ — Colorado/Utah.

### Southwest

- **APA Apachería** ✅ — AZ/NM/W.TX zone.

### Pacific Northwest

### Subarctic

### Culture-only (no tag)
Lenape, Kickapoo, Sauk, Meskwaki, Menominee; Arikara/Mandan/Hidatsa (Upper Missouri villages); Kiowa, Tonkawa, Kansa, Ponca, Quapaw; Bannock, Paiute, Goshute; Cayuse, Walla Walla, Umatilla; Pueblo peoples (under Spanish sovereignty, except autonomous Hopi); Coast Salish, Chinook, Nuu-chah-nulth, Kwakwaka'wakw; Woodland/Swampy Cree, Inuit, Aleut; California groups (Pomo, Wintun, etc.).
