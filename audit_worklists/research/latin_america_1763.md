# Latin America & Caribbean Political Control in 1763
## Ground Truth for Province-Ownership Audit

**Date Context:** February 1763, Treaty of Paris conclusion (Seven Years' War)  
**Purpose:** Paradox mod 1763 scenario province-ownership reference  
**Scope:** Mexico/New Spain through South America + all Caribbean islands

---

## Spanish Colonial Holdings

### Viceroyalty of New Spain (Virreinato de Nueva España)
- **Capital:** Mexico City (Ciudad de México)
- **Territorial Extent:** 
  - Core Mexico (central highlands, Bajío, Oaxaca)
  - Northern frontier (Nueva Vizcaya, Nuevo León, Coahuila, Texas, New Mexico, Alta California, Sonora)
  - Central America south to Costa Rica
  - Caribbean: Cuba, Puerto Rico, Santo Domingo (eastern Hispaniola)
  - Philippines (trans-Pacific)
- **Key Cities:** Mexico City, Veracruz, Puebla, Guadalajara, Guanajuato, Querétaro, Mérida (Yucatán), Guatemala City, Havana, San Juan
- **Classification:** TAG (major colonial administrative unit)
- **1763 Note:** Cuba and Manila returned by Britain per Treaty of Paris; Spanish Florida ceded TO Britain 1763
- **Source:** Burkholder & Johnson, *Colonial Latin America* (Oxford, 2008) — New Spain as largest, richest viceroyalty spanning North America to Central America

### Viceroyalty of Peru (Virreinato del Perú)
- **Capital:** Lima (Los Reyes)
- **Territorial Extent:** 
  - Core Peru (coastal plains, Andean highlands, Cuzco)
  - Upper Peru/Charcas (modern Bolivia — Potosí silver mines)
  - **Río de la Plata region** (modern Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay — NOT yet separate viceroyalty until 1776)
  - Chile (under Captaincy-General, see below, but nominally under Lima)
- **Key Cities:** Lima, Cuzco, Potosí, Arequipa, Buenos Aires, Córdoba, Asunción, Montevideo, Santiago (Chile)
- **Classification:** TAG (major viceroyalty)
- **1763 Note:** CRITICAL — Viceroyalty of Río de la Plata created 1776, so in 1763 Buenos Aires/Paraguay/Uruguay still under Peru's jurisdiction
- **Source:** Fisher, *Bourbon Peru 1750-1824* (Liverpool UP, 2003) — pre-1776 Peru encompassed entire South American Spanish territory except New Granada

### Viceroyalty of New Granada (Virreinato de Nueva Granada)
- **Capital:** Santafé de Bogotá
- **Territorial Extent:**
  - Modern Colombia (Andean core, Caribbean coast)
  - Modern Ecuador (Quito audiencia)
  - Modern Panama (trans-isthmian route)
  - Modern Venezuela (under Captaincy-General, see below, but nominally under Bogotá until 1777)
- **Key Cities:** Bogotá, Cartagena, Quito, Guayaquil, Panamá, Popayán, Caracas
- **Classification:** TAG (viceroyalty, re-established 1739 after brief 1717-1723 experiment)
- **1763 Note:** Venezuela technically under New Granada until Captaincy-General of Venezuela gains independence from viceregal authority 1777
- **Source:** McFarlane, *Colombia Before Independence* (Cambridge, 1993) — 1739 re-establishment consolidated control of northern South America

### Captaincy-General of Cuba (Capitanía General de Cuba)
- **Capital:** Havana (La Habana)
- **Territorial Extent:** Cuba island, subordinate to Viceroyalty of New Spain but with significant military autonomy
- **Key Cities:** Havana, Santiago de Cuba, Trinidad
- **Classification:** TAG or SUB-TAG (militarized captaincy-general, could be modeled as subject of New Spain or independent TAG)
- **1763 Note:** Returned to Spain by Britain in Treaty of Paris (Britain occupied Havana 1762-63)
- **Source:** Kuethe, *Cuba, 1753-1815* (Tennessee, 1986) — captaincy-general with exceptional autonomy due to strategic Caribbean importance

### Captaincy-General of Guatemala (Capitanía General de Guatemala)
- **Capital:** Santiago de los Caballeros de Guatemala (Antigua Guatemala until 1773 earthquake, then Nueva Guatemala)
- **Territorial Extent:** Modern Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Chiapas (Mexico)
- **Key Cities:** Guatemala City, San Salvador, Comayagua, León (Nicaragua), Cartago (Costa Rica)
- **Classification:** TAG or SUB-TAG (subordinate to New Spain but with internal autonomy)
- **Source:** Wortman, *Government and Society in Central America, 1680-1840* (Columbia, 1982) — captaincy-general as distinct administrative unit within New Spain superstructure

### Captaincy-General of Chile (Capitanía General de Chile / Reino de Chile)
- **Capital:** Santiago de la Nueva Extremadura
- **Territorial Extent:** Central Chile from Copiapó to **Biobío River** (southern frontier vs. Mapuche); excludes Araucanía
- **Key Cities:** Santiago, Valparaíso, Concepción (frontier garrison city on Biobío)
- **Classification:** TAG or SUB-TAG (nominally under Peru, functionally autonomous military frontier)
- **1763 Note:** Biobío River = HARD BORDER with independent Mapuche territory (Araucanía) per Spanish parlamentos (treaties)
- **Source:** Villalobos, *Historia del Pueblo Chileno* (Zig-Zag, 1983) — Chile as militarized frontier, Biobío as treaty line with Mapuche

### Captaincy-General of Venezuela (Capitanía General de Venezuela)
- **Capital:** Caracas (Santiago de León de Caracas)
- **Territorial Extent:** Venezuelan coast and llanos, Maracaibo, Guayana
- **Key Cities:** Caracas, La Guaira, Maracaibo, Cumaná, Barcelona
- **Classification:** TAG or SUB-TAG (created 1777 as independent from New Granada, but earlier precursors exist)
- **1763 Note:** In 1763 still nominally under New Granada; full autonomy 1777. Could model as SUB-TAG or fold into New Granada TAG
- **Source:** Ferry, *The Colonial Elite of Early Caracas* (California, 1989) — 1770s autonomy formalized earlier de facto independence from Bogotá

## Portuguese Colonial Holdings

### State of Brazil (Estado do Brasil)
- **Capital:** Salvador da Bahia (until 1763), **Rio de Janeiro (from 1763)**
- **Territorial Extent:**
  - Coastal captaincies from Pará (Amazon mouth) to Rio Grande do Sul
  - Interior: Minas Gerais gold region, São Paulo bandeirante frontier, Mato Grosso
  - Largely coastal-focused; interior Amazon and west sparsely controlled
- **Key Cities:** Rio de Janeiro (new capital 1763), Salvador, Recife/Olinda, São Paulo, Ouro Preto (Vila Rica), Belém (Pará)
- **Classification:** TAG (unified Portuguese America under single governor-general/viceroy from 1720s)
- **1763 Note:** Capital MOVED from Salvador to Rio de Janeiro in 1763 (same year as Treaty of Paris)
- **Source:** Russell-Wood, *The Portuguese Empire 1415-1808* (Johns Hopkins, 1998) — 1763 capital transfer reflected southern economic/strategic shift (gold, Platine frontier)

### State of Maranhão (Estado do Maranhão e Grão-Pará)
- **Capital:** São Luís (Maranhão), later Belém
- **Territorial Extent:** Northern Brazil (Maranhão, Pará, Amazon basin)
- **Classification:** Merged into unified Brazil by 1750s; by 1763 administratively part of State of Brazil
- **1763 Note:** Separate state abolished 1774-1777; by 1763 transitioning to integration with southern Brazil
- **Source:** Alden, *Royal Government in Colonial Brazil* (California, 1968) — Maranhão separate state reduced by 1760s, formalized integration 1774

**Recommendation:** Model as single **POR/BRZ TAG** with capital Rio de Janeiro (1763 switch). Northern/Amazonian interior as CULTURE-ONLY (Tupi, Tapuia, etc.).

## Caribbean Islands by Colonial Power

### French Caribbean
#### Saint-Domingue (western Hispaniola)
- **Capital:** Cap-Français (Le Cap)
- **Classification:** TAG or SUB-TAG (French colony, most valuable in Caribbean due to sugar)
- **Key Cities:** Cap-Français, Port-au-Prince
- **Source:** Dubois, *Avengers of the New World* (Harvard, 2004) — richest colonial possession globally by 1780s, already dominant by 1763

#### Martinique
- **Capital:** Fort-Royal (later Fort-de-France)
- **Classification:** TAG or SUB-TAG
- **1763 Note:** Briefly occupied by Britain 1762, returned by Treaty of Paris
- **Source:** Pritchard, *In Search of Empire* (Cambridge, 2004)

#### Guadeloupe
- **Capital:** Basse-Terre
- **Classification:** TAG or SUB-TAG
- **1763 Note:** Occupied by Britain 1759-63, returned by Treaty of Paris (Britain chose Canada over Guadeloupe)
- **Source:** Pritchard, *In Search of Empire*

#### Minor French islands: Marie-Galante, Désirade, Les Saintes, Saint-Barthélemy (sold to Sweden 1784), Saint-Martin (northern half, shared with Dutch)

### British Caribbean
#### Jamaica
- **Capital:** Spanish Town (administrative), Kingston (commercial, growing)
- **Classification:** TAG or SUB-TAG (major British sugar colony)
- **Source:** Burnard, *Planters, Merchants, and Slaves* (Chicago, 2015)

#### Barbados
- **Capital:** Bridgetown
- **Classification:** TAG or SUB-TAG (oldest, richest British Caribbean colony by 1763)
- **Source:** Menard, *Sweet Negotiations* (Virginia, 2006)

#### Lesser Antilles British: Antigua, St. Kitts, Nevis, Montserrat, Dominica (ceded by France 1763), St. Vincent (ceded 1763), Grenada (ceded 1763), Tobago (disputed, British by 1763)
- **Source:** O'Shaughnessy, *An Empire Divided* (Pennsylvania, 2000) — 1763 Treaty of Paris expanded British Windward Islands

#### The Bahamas
- **Capital:** Nassau (New Providence)
- **Classification:** SUB-TAG or integrated into British Caribbean TAG
- **Source:** Craton & Saunders, *Islanders in the Stream* (Georgia, 1992)

### Dutch Caribbean
#### Curaçao (+ Aruba, Bonaire — ABC islands)
- **Capital:** Willemstad
- **Classification:** SUB-TAG (Dutch West India Company)
- **Source:** Goslinga, *The Dutch in the Caribbean and the Guianas 1680-1791* (Van Gorcum, 1985)

#### Sint Eustatius, Saba, Sint Maarten (southern half)
- **Classification:** Integrate with Curaçao TAG or separate minor TAG
- **Source:** Goslinga

#### Suriname (Dutch Guiana, South American mainland)
- **Capital:** Paramaribo
- **Classification:** TAG (plantation colony, distinct from island holdings)
- **Source:** Goslinga

### Danish Caribbean
#### Danish West Indies (Saint Thomas, Saint John, Saint Croix — modern US Virgin Islands)
- **Capital:** Charlotte Amalie (Saint Thomas)
- **Classification:** SUB-TAG (Danish colonial possession until 1917 US purchase)
- **Source:** Hall, *Slave Society in the Danish West Indies* (Johns Hopkins, 1992)

### Spanish Caribbean
- **Cuba:** see Captaincy-General above
- **Puerto Rico:** Subordinate to Cuba captaincy-general or directly to New Spain; capital San Juan
- **Santo Domingo (eastern Hispaniola):** Spanish colony, capital Santo Domingo; economically marginal, overshadowed by French Saint-Domingue. Briefly ceded to France 1795.
- **Source:** Moya Pons, *The Dominican Republic: A National History* (Markus Wiener, 2010)

## Indigenous TAG-Worthy Polities

### Mapuche / Araucanía
- **Endonym:** Mapuche (collective ethnonym); political units = *rehues* (clans/districts)
- **Territorial Extent:** South-central Chile from **Biobío River** south to Chiloé, extending into Argentine pampas
- **Capital (if modeled as TAG):** None (decentralized); could use Concepción as frontier interface city OR create abstract "Araucanía" tag
- **Key Locations:** Araucanía region between Biobío and Toltén rivers; Valdivia (Spanish exclave, isolated)
- **Classification:** TAG (treaty-recognized independence, Spanish *parlamentos* formalized borders)
- **Justification:** Spanish Crown signed formal treaties (*parlamentos*, e.g., 1641 Quilín, 1726) recognizing Mapuche sovereignty and Biobío border; Araucanía remained independent until 1880s Chilean conquest.
- **Source:** Boccara, *Guerre et ethnogenèse mapuche* (L'Harmattan, 1998) / Zavala, *Los Mapuches del Siglo XVIII* (2008) — *parlamento* system as de facto international diplomacy, Spanish recognition of Mapuche polity

### Miskito Kingdom (Mosquito Coast)
- **Endonym:** Miskitu (people), no unified "kingdom" term pre-contact; British termed it "Mosquito Kingdom"
- **Territorial Extent:** Caribbean coast of modern Nicaragua and Honduras (Mosquito Coast / La Mosquitia)
- **Capital (if modeled as TAG):** No fixed capital; could use British settlement at Bluefields (though anachronistic name, founded later)
- **Key Locations:** Cabo Gracias a Dios, Black River (British settlement, Rio Tinto area)
- **Classification:** TAG (British-allied polity, British protectorate formalized 1740s-1850s)
- **Justification:** Miskito leaders crowned by British as "kings" (e.g., Jeremy I in 1687, succession continued); British maintained alliance and supported Miskito sovereignty against Spanish claims; functioned as autonomous polity with British backing.
- **Source:** Olien, "The Miskito Kings and the Line of Succession," *Journal of Anthropological Research* 39:2 (1983) — British-sponsored Miskito monarchy as political institution with dynastic continuity

## CULTURE-ONLY Stateless Peoples (Not TAG-Worthy)

These groups lack centralized political structures recognizable as "states" for Paradox mod TAG purposes. Model as culture/religion pops in provinces without sovereign control.

### Amazonian Interior Peoples
- **Groups:** Hundreds of distinct ethnicities (Tupi-Guarani, Yanomami, Munduruku, Ticuna, etc.)
- **Extent:** Amazon basin interior (modern Brazil, Peru, Colombia, Venezuela, Guianas hinterlands)
- **Status:** Autonomous village-level societies; no supra-village polities in 1763
- **Source:** Hemming, *Red Gold* (Harvard, 1978) — Amazon peoples organized at village/kin level, no state structures

### Guajira / Wayuu
- **Extent:** Guajira Peninsula (modern Colombia/Venezuela border)
- **Status:** Semi-nomadic pastoralists, clan-based; resisted Spanish control but no unified polity
- **Source:** Polo Acuña, *Etnicidad, conflicto social y cultura fronteriza en La Guajira (1700-1850)* (Universidad de los Andes, 2005)

### Darién Kuna
- **Extent:** Darién jungle (modern Panama/Colombia border)
- **Status:** Autonomous communities, no centralized leadership; Spanish never controlled interior
- **Source:** Howe, *A People Who Would Not Kneel* (Smithsonian, 1998) — Kuna autonomy via geographic inaccessibility, not state formation

### Gran Chaco Peoples (Guaycuru, Wichí, Toba, etc.)
- **Extent:** Gran Chaco lowlands (modern Argentina, Paraguay, Bolivia)
- **Status:** Mobile hunter-gatherers and foragers, resisted Spanish/Portuguese missions; no states
- **Source:** Saeger, *The Chaco Mission Frontier* (Arizona, 2000)

### Patagonian Tehuelche / "Patagones"
- **Extent:** Patagonia steppes south of Río Negro (modern Argentina)
- **Status:** Nomadic hunter-gatherers, band-level societies; minimal European contact in 1763
- **Source:** Casanueva, "Los primeros en Patagonia," in *Historia de la Patagonia* (UNPSJB, 2006)

### Pampas Peoples (Puelche, Het / "Pampas Indians")
- **Extent:** Argentine pampas between Buenos Aires frontier and Patagonia
- **Status:** Mobile groups, some adoption of horses; raids on colonial frontier but no unified polity
- **Source:** Mayo, "El cautiverio y sus funciones en una sociedad de frontera," *Revista de Indias* 45:175 (1985)

### Caribbean / Lesser Antilles Caribs
- **Extent:** Dominica, St. Vincent (pre-1763 French cession to Britain), scattered Lesser Antilles
- **Status:** Autonomous communities, resisted colonization; "Black Caribs" (Garifuna) on St. Vincent formed from Carib + African maroon mixture; no central polity
- **Source:** Hulme & Whitehead, *Wild Majesty: Encounters with Caribs* (Oxford, 1992)

## Key 1763 Anachronisms to Avoid

### DO NOT INCLUDE (Post-1763)
1. **Viceroyalty of Río de la Plata** — created 1776, capital Buenos Aires; in 1763 Buenos Aires under Viceroyalty of Peru
2. **Independent Latin American republics** — all emerge 1810s-1820s (Argentina 1816, Chile 1818, Peru 1821, Mexico 1821, Gran Colombia 1819-1830, Brazil 1822)
3. **Captaincy-General of Venezuela as autonomous** — formalized 1777; in 1763 transitional, nominally under New Granada
4. **United States presence** — no USA until 1776; British Thirteen Colonies north of Florida
5. **Banda Oriental / Uruguay as distinct** — part of Río de la Plata jurisdiction under Peru in 1763; Uruguay emerges 1828
6. **Haiti / Haitian Revolution** — 1791-1804; in 1763 it is French Saint-Domingue
7. **Gran Colombia** — 1819-1831 Bolívarian union; in 1763 separate New Granada + Venezuela
8. **British Guiana, French Guiana distinctions as formalized** — 1763 Guianas still contested (Dutch Suriname clear; French Cayenne established; British claims emerging)

### DO INCLUDE (Pre-1763 or Exactly 1763)
1. **Spanish Florida CEDED to Britain 1763** — Treaty of Paris; by late 1763 British territory
2. **Cuba RETURNED to Spain 1763** — Treaty of Paris; British occupation 1762-63
3. **Guadeloupe, Martinique RETURNED to France 1763** — Treaty of Paris
4. **Dominica, St. Vincent, Grenada, Tobago CEDED to Britain 1763** — Treaty of Paris, former French/neutral Carib islands
5. **Portuguese Brazil capital MOVED to Rio de Janeiro 1763** — same year, separate from war
6. **Jesuits expelled from Portuguese America 1759, Spanish America 1767** — 1763 is between these; Jesuit missions still operating in Spanish territory (Paraguay reductions, etc.)

## Recommended TAG Structure for Mod

### Independent TAGs (Sovereign)
1. **SPA (Spain)** — controls all viceroyalties below OR:
   - **NSP (New Spain)** — Mexico + Central America + Spanish Caribbean
   - **PEU (Peru)** — Peru + Upper Peru + Río de la Plata + Chile (if Chile not separate)
   - **NGA (New Granada)** — Colombia + Ecuador + Panama (+ Venezuela if not separate)
   - **CHL (Chile)** — if modeled as autonomous captaincy-general
   - **VEN (Venezuela)** — if modeled as autonomous captaincy-general (anachronistic for 1763 but plausible)
   - **CUB (Cuba)** — if modeled as autonomous captaincy-general
2. **POR (Portugal)** — controls Brazil below OR:
   - **BRZ (Brazil)** — unified Portuguese America, capital Rio de Janeiro (1763)
3. **FRA (France)** — controls French Caribbean below OR:
   - **SDO (Saint-Domingue)** — French Haiti
   - **MTQ (Martinique)** + **GLP (Guadeloupe)** — Windward/Leeward French
4. **GBR (Britain)** — controls British Caribbean below OR:
   - **JAM (Jamaica)**, **BRB (Barbados)**, etc.
5. **NED (Netherlands)** — controls Dutch Caribbean/Guiana below OR:
   - **CUR (Curaçao)**, **SUR (Suriname)**
6. **DEN (Denmark)** — controls Danish West Indies (minor)
7. **MAP (Mapuche / Araucanía)** — independent indigenous TAG, south of Biobío
8. **MSK (Miskito Kingdom)** — British-allied indigenous TAG, Mosquito Coast

### Subject Relationships
- If modeling viceroyalties as separate TAGs, make them subjects of Spain (SPA overlord)
- If modeling Caribbean islands as separate TAGs, make them subjects of respective European powers OR integrate into single Caribbean TAG per power
- Miskito Kingdom: could be British subject or independent with alliance
- Mapuche: independent (possibly Spanish rivalry/truce)

## Summary for Mod Implementation

**Total TAG Candidates (if maximally granular):**
- Spanish America: 7-8 (New Spain, Peru, New Granada, Chile, Venezuela, Cuba, Guatemala [some could be subjects])
- Portuguese America: 1 (Brazil)
- French Caribbean: 3 (Saint-Domingue, Martinique, Guadeloupe [or unified French Caribbean TAG])
- British Caribbean: 2-4 (Jamaica, Barbados, plus minor islands [or unified British Caribbean TAG])
- Dutch: 2 (Curaçao+islands, Suriname [or unified])
- Danish: 1 (Danish West Indies, minor)
- Indigenous: 2 (Mapuche/Araucanía, Miskito Kingdom)

**TOTAL: ~15-20 TAGs** depending on granularity (fewer if viceroyalties/islands consolidated under European overlords).

**Culture-Only Provinces (no TAG):** Amazonian interior, Gran Chaco, Patagonia, Pampas, Guajira, Darién, uncontacted regions.

**Critical 1763 Details:**
- NO Viceroyalty of Río de la Plata (Buenos Aires under Peru)
- Spanish Florida → British (1763)
- Cuba → Spanish (returned 1763)
- Guadeloupe, Martinique → French (returned 1763)
- Dominica, St. Vincent, Grenada, Tobago → British (ceded 1763)
- Brazil capital = Rio de Janeiro (moved 1763)
- Mapuche independent south of Biobío (treaty border)
- Miskito Kingdom British-allied on Mosquito Coast
