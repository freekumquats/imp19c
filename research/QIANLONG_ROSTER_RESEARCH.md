## QIANLONG COURT HISTORICAL FIGURES (1759 & 1790)

### 1. GRAND COUNCIL (軍機處)

**Fuheng 傅恆** (1720-1770)
- **Active:** 1759 only (died 1770)
- **Role:** Grand Councillor, military commander
- **Significance:** Led multiple Ten Great Campaigns; died leading Burma expedition

**Liu Tongxun 劉統勳** (1700-1773) 
- **Active:** 1759 only (died 1773)
- **Role:** Grand Councillor
- **Significance:** Senior administrator known for integrity; father of Liu Yong

**Yu Minzhong 于敏中** (1714-1779)
- **Active:** 1759 only (died 1779)  
- **Role:** Grand Councillor, chief compiler
- **Significance:** Supervised Siku Quanshu early stages

**Agui 阿桂** (1717-1797)
- **Active:** 1759 AND 1790
- **Role:** Grand Councillor, military commander
- **Significance:** One of the most successful Ten Great Campaigns generals

**Heshen 和珅** (1750-1799)
- **Active:** 1790 only (rose to power late 1770s)
- **Role:** Grand Councillor, chief minister
- **Significance:** Qianlong's favorite; accumulated massive wealth; executed 1799

**Wang Jie 王杰** (1725-1805)
- **Active:** 1759 AND 1790
- **Role:** Grand Councillor (from 1785)
- **Significance:** Upright official who opposed Heshen's corruption

**Liu Yong 劉墉** (1719-1804)
- **Active:** 1759 AND 1790
- **Role:** Grand Councillor, calligrapher
- **Significance:** Famous for integrity and calligraphy; son of Liu Tongxun

### 2. MILITARY COMMANDERS (Ten Great Campaigns)

**Yinjishan 尹繼善** (1696-1771)
- **Active:** 1759 only
- **Role:** Governor-General, military administrator
- **Significance:** Senior frontier official; managed multiple campaigns

**Zhaohui 兆惠** (1708-1764)
- **Active:** 1759 only
- **Role:** General
- **Significance:** Led successful Dzungar and Altishahr campaigns (1755-1759)

**Mingrui 明瑞** (d. 1768)
- **Active:** Dead by 1759 campaigns
- **Role:** General, first Ili General
- **Significance:** Killed in Burma campaign 1768

**Fuk'anggan 福康安** (1754-1796)
- **Active:** 1759 (age 5) AND 1790 (active commander)
- **Role:** General, Grand Councillor
- **Significance:** Led Nepal (1790-1792), Taiwan, Gurkha campaigns; possibly Qianlong's biological son

### 3. SCHOLARS

**Ji Yun 紀昀** (1724-1805)
- **Active:** 1759 AND 1790
- **Role:** Chief editor of Siku Quanshu
- **Significance:** Led the greatest literary compilation project in Chinese history

**Dai Zhen 戴震** (1724-1777)
- **Active:** 1759 only
- **Role:** Philosopher, philologist
- **Significance:** Leading evidential research scholar; Siku Quanshu contributor

**Yuan Mei 袁枚** (1716-1797)
- **Active:** 1759 AND 1790
- **Role:** Poet, retired official
- **Significance:** Major literary figure; garden culture; taught female disciples

**Zhao Yi 趙翼** (1727-1814)
- **Active:** 1759 AND 1790
- **Role:** Historian, poet
- **Significance:** Innovative historical methodology; "Twenty-Two Histories Notes"

**Qian Daxin 錢大昕** (1728-1804)
- **Active:** 1759 AND 1790
- **Role:** Philologist, historian
- **Significance:** Leading evidential research scholar; phonology and textual criticism

### 4. FRONTIER OFFICIALS

**Mingrui 明瑞** - See military commanders (Ili General; died Burma 1768)

**Agui 阿桂** - See Grand Council (served as Ili General)

**Various Tibetan Ambans** - Names not well documented in English sources for this period

### 5. ADVERSARIES/REBELS

**Amursana 阿睦爾撒納** (c.1722-1757)
- **Active:** Dead before 1759
- **Role:** Dzungar prince/rebel
- **Significance:** Led final Dzungar resistance; died of smallpox in Russian exile 1757

**Khoja Brothers (大小和卓)**
- **Burhan al-Din** and **Khoja Jihan** (both d. 1759)
- **Active:** 1759 only (rebellion crushed 1759)
- **Role:** Altishahr Muslim leaders
- **Significance:** Led Uyghur rebellion against Qing; defeated by Zhaohui

**Wang Lun 王倫** (d. 1774)
- **Active:** Between 1759 and 1790 (rebellion 1774)
- **Role:** Religious sect rebel leader
- **Significance:** Led 1774 Shandong Eight Trigrams uprising

**White Lotus Rebellion** (1796-1804)
- **Active:** After 1790 (began 1796)
- **Role:** Multi-province peasant uprising
- **Significance:** Major sign of Qing decline; drained treasury

### 6. CONSORTS/EUNUCHS

**Empress Xiaoxianchun 孝賢純皇后** (Fuca clan, 1712-1748)
- **Active:** Dead before 1759
- **Role:** First Empress of Qianlong
- **Significance:** Qianlong's great love; her death deeply affected him

**Empress Nara 那拉氏** (Step Empress, 1718-1766)
- **Active:** 1759 only (disgraced 1765, died 1766)
- **Role:** Second Empress (1750-1765)
- **Significance:** Mysteriously disgraced during 1765 southern tour

**Imperial Noble Consort Chunhui 純惠皇貴妃** (1713-1760)
- **Active:** 1759 only
- **Role:** Senior consort
- **Significance:** Mother of future Jiaqing Emperor (though he was adopted by Empress Xiaoxianchun)

**Note on Eunuchs:** Qing dynasty maintained strict eunuch suppression; no powerful eunuchs emerged during Qianlong era unlike Ming dynasty.

- **Alive & active 1790:** Agui, Heshen, Wang Jie, Liu Yong, Fuk'anggan, Ji Yun, Yuan Mei, Zhao Yi, Qian Daxin
