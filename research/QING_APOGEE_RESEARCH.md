# QING APOGEE — research report (early_bookmark #194)

Compiled by the peak-Qing research agent (EN+ZH scholarly historiography, cross-checked;
grounded by three verification sub-agents on treasury, population/territory, and court roster).
Full biographical roster: see `QIANLONG_ROSTER_RESEARCH.md`.

## HEADLINE ANSWER: **1759** (乾隆二十四年)

The year the Qing completed the conquest of the Tarim Basin (Altishahr/回部) with the capture
of the Khoja brothers in Badakhshan (1 Sept 1759), consummating the destruction of the Dzungar
Khanate (1755). **Maximal effective territorial extent + militarily unchallenged + emperor
(Hongli, age 47) at peak vigour + treasury deep + nothing yet foreshadowing decline.** Crest of
the 康雍乾盛世 (High Qing).

**Alternative: c.1790** — "Twilight of the Golden Age": population/cartographic maximum (~300M,
~14.7M km² after Nepal 1792), but Heshen's corruption at full throttle and White Lotus (1796)
only 6 years off. EN scholarship → 1759 (territory/power; Perdue, Rowe, Elliott); ZH scholarship
→ 1770s–1790s (prosperity/population; Dai Yi 戴逸, Guo Chengkang 郭成康, "盛世危機").
Compromise midpoint c.1775 (treasury peak ~73.9M taels; Siku Quanshu begins 1772).

**Decision (this build): 1759** — the research's primary recommendation; a dated, decisive,
unambiguous anchor. 1790 remains a one-branch alternative if the user prefers the decadence flavour.

## REIGNING EMPEROR (1759)
弘曆 Hongli, the **乾隆 Qianlong** Emperor. Temple 高宗 Gaozong. Born 25 Sep 1711; acceded 1735;
abdicated 1796 (太上皇); died 1799 (aged 87). **Age 47 in 1759.** High admin + high military +
high scholarly (scholar-emperor). → `char:214` already in `00_Qing.txt` (b.1711.09.25, d.1799.02.07,
`add_trait=chinese_emperor`). Alive for both 1759 and 1790.

## TERRITORY (1759) — **essentially identical to the 1815 baseline**
China proper (18 provinces), Manchuria, Inner+Outer Mongolia, **Xinjiang (freshly conquered
1755–59)**, Tibet (protectorate since 1720, ambans at Lhasa), Qinghai (1724), Taiwan (1683),
SW gaitu-guiliu zone (ongoing). **No cessions occurred 1759→1815** (first losses = 1858/1860 Amur,
1864/1881 Ili — all post-1815). White Lotus (1796–1804) was internal, changed no borders.
→ **The 1759 and 1815 maps are nearly the same; the bookmark contrast is internal (vigour,
treasury, army quality, prestige), NOT territorial.** Absolute cartographic max (~14.7M km²) dates
to c.1790 (after Nepal); 1759 marginally smaller only at the Himalayan fringe.

**Tributaries (1759):** Korea (model tributary), Ryukyu (dual Qing+Satsuma subordination), Vietnam/
Annam, Siam, Laos/Sulu (intermittent). **Nepal is NOT yet a tributary in 1759** (becomes 5-yearly
only after the 1792 Treaty of Betrawati — also true for 1790). **Burma & Kokand: contested** — Qing
claimed tributary status, modern scholarship doubts it; do NOT add.

## POPULATION / ECONOMY / TREASURY (1759)
- Population ~190–200M registered in 1759 (1750≈181.8M reg / ~225M est; 1800≈297.6M reg / ~330M est;
  1790≈300M). CONTESTED — registered≠headcount (Ho Ping-ti 何炳棣, 1959). Treat as estimate.
- Treasury (戶部銀庫): Kangxi d.1722 ~27.2M → Yongzheng d.1735 ~34M → **Qianlong peak ~73.9M taels
  mid-1770s** (ZH: ~70–80M c.1777–81). **1759 = large & rising surplus** (well above 34M, climbing to
  the mid-1770s peak; exact 1759 balance not pinned). Collapsed to ~19M by early Jiaqing (White Lotus).
- Canton System (廣州制度 / Cohong 公行) funnels net silver INFLOW (tea/silk for New World silver) —
  the opium-driven reversal is post-1815 (another apogee-vs-1815 contrast). Salt monopoly (鹽政) a
  cash cow. New World crops drive the population boom. 攤丁入畝 (tanding-rudi, head-tax merged into
  land tax; 1712 freeze / 1717 Guangdong / 1726–29 rollout) is the operative fiscal regime by 1759.

## INSTITUTIONS (1759) — the "before-decay" state
軍機處 Grand Council at mature apex (nerve-centre, running the war). Six Boards 六部 (Manchu+Han
dyarchy). **八旗 Eight Banners at strength** (just conquered Inner Asia) + 綠營 Green Standard still
functional — key contrast with 1815's decayed army. Tribute system confident (pre-Macartney-1793,
pre-Opium-War). 科舉 exams at full strength; 考證學 at intellectual height. Bimetallic copper-cash/
silver-tael currency. Projects: Old Summer Palace 圓明園 (Xiyang Lou 西洋樓 built c.1747–60);
Siku Quanshu 四庫全書 (1772–82, after 1759 — complete under a 1790 bookmark); southern tours 南巡.

## EVENTS BRACKETING 1759
Before: 1755 first Zunghar campaign; 1755–57 Amursana revolt+suppression; 1757 Canton System
formalized; 1758–59 Altishahr/Khoja campaign. **Anchor: 1 Sep 1759** Khoja brothers run down in
Badakhshan — conquest of Xinjiang complete. After: 1760 birth of future Jiaqing; 1762 Ili General
(伊犁將軍) established; 1765–69 costly failed Burma war (first check); 1771–76 Second Jinchuan;
1772–82 Siku Quanshu; ~1775+ rise of Heshen (hinge to decline).

## ADDENDUM — 2026-07-08 — teammate research-agent corroborations (sourced)

Fed in by parallel research agents during the bookmark build; recorded here so the sourced
numbers survive and inform future year-swaps.

### Population (何炳棣 Ho Ping-ti + 中文 Wikipedia)
- **1741** (乾隆6): 143,411,559 — the census-methodology hinge year: 攤丁入畝 (tandi-rudi) merged
  the head-tax into the land-tax and the count shifted from 丁 (adult-male registration, severe
  undercount) to full 保甲 household enumeration (10 户=牌, 10 牌=甲, 10 甲=保), so all residents
  (women/children/elderly) were registered.
- **1790** (乾隆55): ~301,000,000.  **1794** (乾隆59): 313,281,795.
- **1762 total: NOT directly attested** in the source (article jumps 1741→1790). For a 1759/1762
  bookmark, interpolate ~180–200M (post-1741-reform surge, mixing genuine growth + previously
  hidden population surfacing). Source: 何炳棣《1368–1953中國人口研究》(葛剑雄 譯, 上海古籍 1989, pp.262/268).
- **Implication for the sim:** the mod's pop counts are engine-derived, not a single national total,
  so no direct edit — but this confirms the High-Qing demographic peak sits mid-to-late Qianlong,
  reinforcing 1759 as "power apogee" vs ~1790 "demographic/fiscal apogee" (the D3 framing).

### Ili General (伊犁將軍) — 1762, a ~3-year anachronism at a 1759 start ⚠️ (minor, accepted)
Corroborated: the office was formally established **1762** (Wikipedia infobox says 1759 but the
article body says 1762), unifying military+civil authority over all Xinjiang N & S of the Tianshan
(Zhunbu/Dzungaria 天山北路; Huibu/Altishahr 天山南路; Tianshan Donglu), reporting to the 理藩院.
- The 1815 setup ships `ILI` as an `autonomous_governorship` subject of CHI (00_default.txt). At a
  1759.9.1 start this governorship technically predates the office by ~3 years (Sep 1759 the region
  is just-conquered, administered under interim military command until the 1762 formalization).
- **Decision (consistent with D4 "no territorial/subject change" + D-SCOPE):** LEAVE ILI as-is. A
  3-year institutional-timing gap is a negligible, defensible abstraction (the territory WAS held
  and militarily governed from 1759); removing/reworking it risks the subject/economy bookkeeping
  for no real gain. Flagged only so a future stricter bookmark (or a 1762 start) can revisit.

### Tributary ring — Laos & the SE-Asian churn (confirms D4 "leave the ring as-is")
- **Laos/Vientiane in 1759–90 was NOT a reliable Qing tributary:** Qing tribute was nominal (~once
  per 10 yrs); the real suzerains in this window were **Burma (Konbaung) 1765–79** then **Siam
  (Taksin) 1779–1828**. So NOT adding a Laos dependency to CHI is correct.
- **gaitu-guiliu (改土歸流):** the native-chieftain→career-bureaucrat conversion (SW China, Yi/Nuosu
  etc.) was largely a Yongzheng-era (1720s–30s) push, essentially complete/ongoing background by
  1759 — already abstracted by the mod's ethnic-tension layer (se_QING_ETHNIC_TENSION), no setup edit.
  Source: Jan Karlach, J. Chinese History 10:1 (Jan 2026) 131–165, DOI 10.1017/jch.2024.54.
