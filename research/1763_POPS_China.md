# Qing China Population c.1763 (mid-Qianlong) — research basis for #284

Sourcing: Ho Ping-ti 何炳棣 *Studies on the Population of China, 1368–1953* (1959); Cao Shuji 曹樹基
《中国人口史·明清卷》; William Rowe *China's Last Empire* (2009); Peter Perdue *China Marches West* (2005);
Liang Fangzhong 梁方仲《中国历代户口、田地、田赋统计》; registered 民數/丁 returns (清實錄/戶部).
1763 is NOT a discrete registration year — bracketed by the 1749/1766/1776 民數 returns and interpolated.
Flags: [registered] = the 民數/丁 fiscal return; [estimate] = modern scholarly reconstruction.

## 1. Total population c.1763
Best modern estimate: **~205–215 million actual** for China proper c.1760–1765 (range ~185–225M). Use
**~210M** as the headline. Registered series [registered]: 1749 ~177M, 1750 ~181.8M, 1766 ~208M, 1776 ~268M
(jump = better registration, not real growth), 1790 ~301M.
CAVEAT — ding-vs-head reform: pre-1740s counted *ding* 丁 (fiscal adult-male units), NOT people; the
攤丁入地 tanding-rudi reform (empire-wide by 1740s) + the 1741 baojia 保甲 order to report total persons 民數
is why "population" leaps ~140M(1741)→200M+ within a generation. Pre-1741 ding = lower bound only.
Mod: ~185M for "registered" feel, ~205–215M for actual demographic weight.

## 2. Provincial distribution (China proper) [estimate, Cao Shuji-style, treat as magnitudes/shares]
Ranked approx pop c.1770s (millions): Jiangsu ~30 (Jiangnan core, highest density on earth), Shandong ~22–24,
Zhejiang ~18–21, Guangdong ~16–19, Henan ~16–18, Anhui ~16–18, Jiangxi ~15–17, Zhili/Hebei ~14–17 (Beijing),
Hubei ~14–16, Hunan ~13–16, Fujian ~10–12, Shanxi ~10–12, Gansu ~8–12 (large Hui pop), Shaanxi ~7–9,
Sichuan ~6–8 (recovering), Guangxi ~5–7 (many non-Han), Yunnan ~4–6 (frontier), Guizhou ~4–5 (Miao frontier).
Dense core = Lower Yangzi/Jiangnan (Jiangsu+Zhejiang+S.Anhui) + North China Plain (Shandong/Henan/S.Zhili).
Rising secondary = Middle Yangzi (Hubei/Hunan/Jiangxi) + Lingnan (Guangdong/Pearl delta).
Frontier/low-density = SW (Yunnan/Guizhou/Guangxi), NW (Gansu/W.Shaanxi), Sichuan (refilling).
SICHUAN flag: Ming-Qing collapse ~4M(c1600)→~0.8M(c1660, ~80% collapse); 湖廣填四川 migration refilled it:
~4M(1720s)→~8M(1776)→~22M(1813). In 1763 = rapidly-infilling immigrant frontier ~6–7M, mostly Han from
Hubei/Hunan/Jiangxi (in-migration mechanic candidate).

## 3. Largest cities c.1763 [estimate]
Beijing ~0.9–1.0M (political #1, among the world's largest); Suzhou ~0.5–1M metro (economic/cultural #1 of
Jiangnan); Hangzhou ~0.5M; Nanjing ~0.5M+; Guangzhou/Canton ~0.5–0.8M metro (sole legal Western port under
the 1757 Canton System — trade gateway); Wuhan cities (Hankou+Wuchang+Hanyang) ~0.5M+ (mid-Yangzi entrepôt).
Others: Yangzhou (salt), Foshan (iron/ceramics), Jingdezhen (porcelain), Tianjin, Xi'an, Chengdu, Fuzhou.

## 4. Frontier & ethnic periphery [estimate, lightly/unregistered]
- Manchuria (盛京/吉林/黑龍江): ~1–2M, thin, mostly southern (Fengtian/Liaodong). Han settlers south (despite
  ban), Manchu bannermen + homeland garrisons, Tungusic (Sibe/Daur/Solon), some Mongols. Han folk-religion;
  Manchu shamanism + Tibetan Buddhism among elites.
- Inner Mongolia ~1–2M Mongols (pastoral); Outer/Khalkha few-hundred-K to ~1M. Mongol; Tibetan (Gelug)
  Buddhism, heavily monastic (large share of adult males lamas).
- Xinjiang/Ili — DEPOPULATION flag: the Dzungar Khanate (~600K, Wei Yuan) was annihilated 1755–58 (~70–80%
  died: ~40% smallpox, ~30% killed, ~20% fled). So 1763 Dzungaria (N. Xinjiang) is near-emptied steppe just
  starting Qing resettlement (Han tuntian around Urumqi, Hui, Manchu/Sibe garrisons, Taranchi Uyghur farmers).
  Tarim Basin (Altishahr, S. Xinjiang) was populous: ~250–300K+ households of Turkic-Muslim oasis farmers =
  Uyghurs, Sunni Islam. Total Xinjiang well under 1M in 1763, unusually ethnically fragmented.
- Tibet ~1–2M (common low anchor ~1M). Tibetan; Tibetan (Gelug/Vajrayana) Buddhism, very monastic. Qing =
  small garrison (~2–3K) + amban, not settlement.
- SW non-Han (Yunnan/Guizhou/Guangxi/W.Hunan): several million Miao/Yi/Zhuang/Dai/Bouyei/Yao interspersed
  with expanding Han. 改土歸流 gaitu guiliu zone (hereditary 土司 → bureaucratic admin, accelerated under
  Yongzheng/Ortai 1720s–30s), recurrent Miao revolts. Indigenous/animist + encroaching Han syncretism.

## 5. Ethnic/religious map (per-region pop culture+religion)
| Region | Ethnicity | Religion |
|---|---|---|
| Lower Yangzi, N.China Plain, SE coast, most 18 provinces | Han | Confucian–Mahayana–Daoist syncretism |
| Sichuan | Han (recent Huguang immigrants) | Han syncretic |
| SW uplands (Guizhou/Yunnan/Guangxi/W.Hunan) | Han + Miao/Yi/Zhuang/Dai | Han syncretic + indigenous/animist |
| NW (Gansu/W.Shaanxi/Ningxia) | Han + Hui (Sino-Muslim) | Han syncretic + Sunni Islam |
| Manchuria | Han (south) + Manchu bannermen + Tungusic | Han syncretic; Manchu shamanism + Tibetan Buddhism |
| Inner & Outer Mongolia | Mongol | Tibetan Buddhism (Gelug), monastic |
| Xinjiang Tarim (Altishahr) | Uyghur/Turkic Muslim | Sunni Islam |
| Xinjiang Dzungaria (N/Ili) | depopulated → Han/Hui/Taranchi/Sibe/Manchu | mixed (Islam+Han+Buddhism) |
| Tibet | Tibetan | Tibetan Buddhism (Gelug), monastic |
Rule of thumb: ~95%+ of the empire's people are Han in the 18 provinces; frontier dependencies are only a few
million combined but are the ethnically/religiously distinctive regions.

## Confidence
HIGH: total-pop framing + ding-reform caveat (Ho Ping-ti); Beijing ~1M; Sichuan collapse/refill (Entenmann);
Dzungar genocide magnitudes (Perdue/Wei Yuan). MEDIUM/reconstructed: the provincial table (shares/magnitudes,
not verbatim — for exact per-province 民數 pull Liang Fangzhong + Cao Shuji vol.5). ROUGH: frontier numbers
(Tibet/Mongolia/Manchuria magnitudes reliable, precise figures not). NOT FOUND: a clean 1763-specific
provincial breakdown (1763 not a return year).

## How this maps to the mod data (from the recon pass)
Pops live as strata `amount=` blocks in 19 setup/provinces/00_*.txt (BOM/LF): lower/middle/upper_strata,
proletariat, tribesmen, slaves, indentured. Province-level culture=/religion= is the per-strata default; a
strata block may carry its own culture=/religion= to make a minority pop. Total pop = sum of amount= (no
size field); scale a province by multiplying its amount= values. governorship_population (runtime aggregate
of amount= sums) drives food/luxury/economy demand — so scaling amount= feeds economy directly. CHI literacy
floors already set in se_EDU.txt (t1=0.17, t2=0.01). CHI owns ~450 provinces via own_control_core in
00_default.txt (line ~35651); ~573 province blocks across the 19 files (subset owned by CHI vs autonomous
governorships MNC/MKD/HLJ). CHI poptype_rights already bands beihua/wu/yue/xiang etc. by strata.
