### CANDIDATE 1: Full Imperial Examination System (科舉) Deep Sim
**Historical scope:** The imperial examination system was the engine of Qing governance—三年大比 triennial provincial (舉人), metropolitan (貢士), and palace (進士) exams selecting officials from ~1.4M scholars. At 1763 = zenith, functionally meritocratic; by 1815 = corrupted (捐納 purchase, examiner bribery). Currently ABSTRACTED in `se_QING_GOVERNANCE.txt` as a single `qing_exam_ladder` counter (60-baseline, integrity+effectiveness→target, corruption-drag) feeding into `qing_bureau_integrity`. This is the MINIMUM viable model—real engine demands CONCRETE: shengyuan/juren/jinshi on-map scholar characters, triennial exam cycles (1765/68/71…), provincial/metropolitan/palace phases each a player interaction event, success-rate tuned by corruption/council/bureau, graduates enter a hiring pool for offices, exam-stoppage crises (Taiping 1850-64 = 13 years no exams→generational talent gap), reform-era abolition (1905) as a capstone.

**Why it fits:** (1) Existing `qing_exam_ladder` var is a STUB begging for promotion to full system. (2) Proven idioms: create_character for scholars (already used for anachronistic-spawn), set_as_minor_character for the failed-exam masses, on-map office-holder pool reuses Grand Council appointment engine (`se_QING_PERSONNEL.txt`), triennial pulse via dated-event pattern (#303 ARW/FrenchRev schedulers). (3) HUGE gameplay depth: player-facing exam-policy decisions (lower pass threshold→more graduates→dilute quality vs tighten→talent shortage; enable 捐納 for revenue vs preserve integrity; provincial-quota reforms; 廢科舉 abolition timing). (4) Links many shallow systems: exam-ladder → bureau-staffing → council-effectiveness → faction-balance (reformers push abolition, conservatives defend tradition). (5) Historical grounding: the CORE of Qing governance; no Victorian China sim is credible without dramatizing it.

**Scope (files):** NEW `common/scripted_effects/se_QING_EXAM.txt` (QING_exam_init, QING_exam_trigger_cycle, QING_exam_phase_provincial/metropolitan/palace, QING_exam_generate_cohort, QING_exam_graduate, QING_exam_fail, QING_exam_hire_pool_add, QING_exam_crisis_suspend, QING_exam_reform_abolish); NEW `events/imp19c_mod_events/qing_exam_events.txt` (qing_exam.1 provincial phase offer, .2 metropolitan, .3 palace, .4 corruption scandal, .5 quota-reform memorial, .6 捐納-enable, .7 abolition debate, .8 talent-shortage crisis); extend `se_QING_GOVERNANCE.txt` (replace abstract `qing_exam_ladder` feed with concrete grad-count read from hire-pool); extend `se_QING_PERSONNEL.txt` (office appointment checks hire-pool first); NEW `common/scripted_guis/QING_exam_panel.txt` (L4 exam-policy GUI: toggle 捐納, set provincial quotas, trigger abolition); extend `on_action/economy/oa_economy_setup.txt` (triennial cycle scheduler @1765/68/71…); localization; modifiers for exam-policy states.

### CANDIDATE 4: Silver/Opium Balance-of-Trade Monetary Model (白銀外流 / 鴉片貿易)
**Historical scope:** The Qing monetary system was a silver-standard (白銀本位)—taxes/trade/wages denominated in taels, with copper-cash subsidiary. From ~1760s onward, a MASSIVE silver-drain began: China exported tea/silk/porcelain (trade-surplus) but the West had nothing China wanted (the Canton-system "unfavorable balance"), SO Western merchants PAID IN SILVER (Mexican/Spanish dollars flooded China). Post-1820s this REVERSED: British smuggled Indian opium (鴉片) into China (10k chests/yr 1820 → 40k chests/yr 1838 per Immanuel Hsü), draining ~10M taels/yr OUT of China, causing DEFLATION + currency-crisis + Lin Zexu's 1839 Guangzhou opium-destruction → First Opium War. Currently PARTIALLY MODELED: (1) A generic `se_CURRENCY_STRESS.txt` engine exists (currency-health meter, reserve-ratio-impact drift, band-classification), factored from Qing; (2) `se_QING_DECLINE.txt` has a `qing_currency_stress` counter (0-100) that feeds into decline pulse; (3) Opium EXISTS as a trade_good in `se_DEMAND.txt` (demand-from-luxury calls); (4) A silver-standard backing_type in `se_CURRENCY.txt`. BUT MISSING THE TRADE-FLOW MODEL: per-good trade-balance calculation (tea/silk/porcelain EXPORTS earn silver-inflow; opium IMPORTS drain silver-outflow), opium-prohibition policy (Lin Zexu 1839 crackdown vs tolerate-for-revenue), treaty-port opium-legalization (1858 Tianjin treaty), addiction-rate pop mechanic (opium-consuming pops → social-unrest + productivity-drag), smuggling-vs-legal opium split (pre-treaty = 100% smuggled, post-treaty = legal but taxed).

**Why it fits:** (1) Existing currency-stress engine is GENERIC infrastructure begging for a CONCRETE trade-flow feed (right now it just drifts from reserve-ratio, not actual opium/tea flows). (2) Proven idioms: trade-good regional-production sums (already used in `se_TRADE.txt` quarterly pulse), numeric-var balance arithmetic (inflow - outflow = net), pop-type iteration (se_PRODUCTION.txt pops loops), modifier-bands for addiction-tiers (qing_opium_epidemic_low / _high, same as qing_corruption bands).
