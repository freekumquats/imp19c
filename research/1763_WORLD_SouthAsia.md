# South Asia — 16 February 1763

**Research Target**: Indian subcontinent + Ceylon, 1763-02-16 snapshot for Imperator: Rome total-conversion mod re-targeting from 1815 start.

**Key Context**: This is the world BEFORE British territorial raj; 1763 = post-Panipat (1761) Maratha collapse, collapsing Mughal center, rising regional successor states, European coastal trading companies only. Delta vs 1815 (EIC-dominated India) is MASSIVE.

## I. MUGHAL EMPIRE 1763 — Nominal Authority, Real Collapse

### Emperor Shah Alam II (r. 1759-1806)

**STATUS in 1763**: Fugitive emperor, powerless puppet shuttling between warlords.

**Timeline to 1763**:
- **1759**: Proclaimed emperor while in exile in Bihar/Bengal after father Alamgir II's assassination
- **1760-1764**: Under protection (effective captivity) of Nawab Mir Qasim of Bengal, then Nawab Shuja-ud-Daula of Awadh
- **16 Feb 1763**: Shah Alam II is at Patna/Bihar region, nominally under Awadh protection, attempting to extract tribute from EIC-controlled Bengal
- **Coming**: Will fight at Buxar (Oct 1764) alongside Awadh + Mir Qasim against EIC, lose catastrophically, then become EIC pensioner

**Real Power**: NONE. The Mughal Empire exists as:
1. **Symbolic legitimacy**: Regional rulers still sought Mughal titles (Nizam, Nawab, Subadar) for legitimation
2. **Tiny direct territory**: Delhi + immediate surroundings under Maratha/Rohilla warlord control, nominal Mughal writ
3. **Red Fort court**: A pensioner institution; real power held by whoever controlled Delhi (Marathas 1757-1761, then brief chaos, then Afghan/Rohilla influence)

**Key Sources**:
- Jadunath Sarkar, *Fall of the Mughal Empire* Vol. II (1932-1950) — definitive on Shah Alam II's wanderings
- William Irvine, *Later Mughals* (1922) — English chronicle
- Ghulam Husain Tabatabai, *Siyar-ul-Mutakhkherin* (سیر المتاخرین, c.1781-82, Persian) — contemporary Persian chronicle by a Mughal official who witnessed the collapse; CRITICAL primary source

**Territorial Reality 1763**:
- **Delhi**: Under weak Maratha/Jat/Rohilla contested control after Panipat chaos; Mughals = ceremonial
- **Agra**: Jat power (Suraj Mal, d. 1763)
- **Provinces (Subahs)**: Nominally Mughal, actually independent successor states (see below)

## II. MARATHA CONFEDERACY 1763 — Post-Panipat Recovery

### The Third Battle of Panipat (14 January 1761) — Catastrophic Defeat

**Context**: Ahmad Shah Durrani (Abdali) of Afghanistan invaded, met Maratha expedition under Sadashivrao Bhau (Peshwa's cousin) at Panipat, 100km north of Delhi. Maratha defeat was TOTAL:
- ~40,000-70,000 Maratha dead (soldiers + camp followers)
- Peshwa Balaji Baji Rao (Nana Saheb) died of shock hearing the news (June 1761)
- Leadership vacuum, financial ruin, confederacy nearly dissolved

**AT 1763**: Recovery phase under new Peshwa **Madhavrao I** (र. 1761-1772, Marathi: माधवराव पेशवे).

### Peshwa Madhavrao I (b. 1745, r. 1761-1772)

**Status 1763**: 
- Appointed Peshwa at age 16 after father's death
- First two years (1761-63) = consolidation, suppressing internal dissent, restoring finances
- By 1763: Beginning reassertion of Maratha power in North India, but NOT yet the restored confederacy of 1770s

**Capital**: **Pune** (Poona), Maharashtra — Peshwa seat

**Confederacy Structure** (1763 reality):

The Maratha polity = **loose confederacy** under Peshwa's nominal leadership, with great noble houses (Sardars) holding semi-independent power bases:

1. **Peshwa (at Pune)** — Chief Minister of Chhatrapati (Maratha king), but real executive power; by 1760s Peshwa > Chhatrapati
   - **Chhatrapati**: Nominal Maratha king at Satara (Shahu's successors, powerless by 1763)
   - **Madhavrao I** = Peshwa, actual confederacy head

2. **Scindia (Shinде, Marathi: शिंदे)** — Gwalior base
   - **1763 head**: Mahadji Scindia rising (b. 1730, will dominate 1770s-90s), but at 1763 still recovering from Panipat (wounded there)
   - **Territory**: Gwalior, Malwa, northern Deccan; Delhi influence coming (post-1763)

3. **Holkar (होळकर)** — Indore base
   - **1763 head**: Ahilyabai Holkar (अहिल्याबाई होळकर, r. 1767-1795 as regent/queen) — but in 1763 her husband Khande Rao (d. 1754) and son (d. 1767) succession; Malhar Rao Holkar I (founder, d. 1766) still alive, leading house
   - **Territory**: Malwa, Indore

4. **Gaekwad (Gaikwad, गायकवाड)** — Baroda base
   - **1763 head**: Damaji Gaekwad (r. 1740s-1768)
   - **Territory**: Gujarat, Baroda

5. **Bhonsle (भोसले) of Nagpur** — Eastern confederacy
   - **1763 head**: Janoji Bhonsle (r. 1755-1772)
   - **Territory**: Nagpur, Berar, Orissa raiding/tribute rights

**1763 Confederacy Situation**:
- **Financially devastated**: Panipat cost enormous wealth (ransoms, lost plunder, army rebuilding)
- **Northern expansion halted**: Durrani victory meant Maratha retreat from Afghanistan/Punjab/Delhi axis (temporarily)
- **Internal conflict**: Great houses semi-autonomous, Peshwa reasserting control
- **Southern secure**: Deccan/Maharashtra = Maratha heartland, stable

**Key Sources**:
- G.S. Sardesai, *New History of the Marathas* (3 vols, 1946-48) — standard English
- Stewart Gordon, *The Marathas 1600-1818* (1993) — modern synthesis
- V.K. Rajwade, *Marāṭhyāñcā Itihāsa* (मराठ्यांचा इतिहास, Marathi, 1915-25) — foundational Marathi scholarship
- *Selections from the Peshwa Daftar* (Peshwa's Persian/Marathi records, published series) — primary documents

### B. BENGAL — Post-Plassey EIC Control

**Context**: Battle of Plassey (1757) = EIC's Clive defeated Nawab Siraj-ud-Daulah, installed puppet Mir Jafar; by 1763 = EIC *de facto* controls Bengal's revenues, Nawab = figurehead.

**Nawab in 1763**: **Mir Qasim** (r. 1760-1763, until Oct 1763)

**Timeline**:
- **1760**: EIC deposed Mir Jafar (too independent), installed Mir Qasim (promised compliant)
- **1762-63**: Mir Qasim tries to assert autonomy, moves capital from Murshidabad to Munger (Bihar), abolishes EIC trade privileges
- **16 Feb 1763**: Rising tensions; Mir Qasim preparing for confrontation with EIC
- **July 1763**: Open war (EIC vs Mir Qasim)
- **Oct 1763**: Mir Qasim flees, EIC reinstalls Mir Jafar
- **1764**: Battle of Buxar (EIC vs Mir Qasim + Awadh + Shah Alam II) = decisive EIC victory, full territorial control

**Territory**: 
- **Bengal proper**: Murshidabad (capital), Dhaka, Calcutta (EIC HQ)
- **Bihar**: Patna, Munger (Mir Qasim's 1763 base)
- **Orissa**: Nominal, contested with Marathas

**Real Power**: EIC controls revenues (*diwani* rights coming formally 1765, but *de facto* after Plassey)

**Key Sources**:
- P.J. Marshall, *Bengal: The British Bridgehead* (1987)

### G. SIKH MISLS — Emerging Confederacy

**Status 1763**: Khalsa Sikh misls (ਮਿਸਲ, military confederacies) forming in Punjab after Durrani withdrawal post-Panipat.

**Context**: 
- Guru Gobind Singh (d. 1708) → Khalsa militant Sikhism
- 1740s-60s: Sikh guerilla bands (*jathas*) coalescing into 12 misls
- **1763**: Early phase, no unified Sikh state yet; misls raiding/controlling Punjab pockets as Durrani power wanes

**Major Misls forming**:

4. **Kanhaiya**, **Nakkai**, **Dalewalia**, etc. — 12 total misls

**Territory 1763**: 
- **No fixed state**: Misls = mobile warrior bands, controlling villages/towns, paying no tribute to Mughals/Durranis
- **Heartland**: Amritsar (Sikh religious center), raiding Lahore/Punjab cities

**Key Sources**:

## IV. EUROPEAN COMPANIES — 16 February 1763

### A. BRITISH EAST INDIA COMPANY (EIC)

**STATUS 1763**: Dominant, post-Plassey (1757) controlling Bengal's revenues; **SEVEN YEARS' WAR JUST ENDING** (Treaty of Paris signed 10 Feb 1763, 6 days before target date).

**Territorial Control**:
1. **Bengal Presidency** (capital: Calcutta/Fort William)
   - *De facto* ruler of Bengal, Bihar, Orissa after Plassey
   - Nawab = puppet (Mir Qasim about to rebel)
   - **Revenue control**: £2-3 million annual from Bengal land revenue (formal *diwani* 1765, but extracting since 1757)

2. **Madras Presidency** (Fort St. George)
   - **Coromandel coast**: Madras, Northern Circars (leased from Nizam 1759)
   - **Carnatic**: Puppet Nawab Muhammad Ali

3. **Bombay Presidency** (tiny 1763)
   - Bombay island only, minimal hinterland

**Military**: 
- Company sepoy armies (native infantry + European officers)
- Bengal Army = strongest (post-Plassey dominance)

**Key Figure**: Robert Clive (governor 1757-60, 1765-67; in England during 1763)

## VI. ECONOMY c.1763 — Pre-Industrial Handicraft, by Region

**CONTEXT**: South Asia c.1763 = world's largest manufacturing economy (textiles, steel, shipbuilding), BUT pre-industrial (handicraft production, no factories). EIC/Europeans = merchants buying Indian goods for export, NOT producers yet.

**Revenue basis**: Land tax (Mughal *zamindari*/*ryotwari* systems inherited by successor states); internal trade taxes; customs on European companies.

---

### A. BENGAL (includes Bihar, Orissa)

**Capital/Centers**: Murshidabad (capital), Dhaka, Patna, Calcutta (EIC), Murshidabad

#### Trade Goods & Locations:

1. **Textiles** (PRIMARY):
   - **Dhaka muslin** (ঢাকাই মসলিন): Ultra-fine cotton, world-famous luxury; **Dhaka** (Bengal) = production center
   - **Raw silk**: Murshidabad, Kasimbazar; Bengal silk = Chinese/Persian market export
   - **Cotton piece-goods**: Plain calicos, chintzes; widespread Bengal/Bihar production

2. **Saltpetre** (potassium nitrate, gunpowder ingredient):
   - **Bihar**: Patna, Gaya, Bihar Sharif = major production (nitrate beds in Ganges plain)
   - Monopoly controlled by EIC post-Plassey (military strategic good)

3. **Opium**:
   - **Bihar**: Patna region = cultivation (poppy); **1763 = small-scale** (Bengal opium boom = 1780s-1820s when EIC monopolizes for China trade)
   - Some Malwa opium (central India) competing

4. **Indigo**:
   - **Bengal/Bihar**: Indigo plantations (blue dye); exported to Europe
   - **1763 = established**, will expand massively under EIC (1770s-1800s plantation system)

5. **Rice**:
   - **Bengal delta**: Surplus rice, internal + coastal trade (Orissa, Coromandel)

6. **Other**: 
   - **Sugar**: Bengal cane sugar (small export)
   - **Lac** (लाख): Bihar/Bengal forests (shellac, dye)

**Economic Status 1763**: 
- **Richest region**: Bengal land revenue = £2-3 million (EIC extracting)
- **EIC trade monopoly**: Post-Plassey, EIC controls export trade (textiles, saltpetre, silk to Britain/China)
- **Weavers**: Murshidabad, Dhaka, Kasimbazar = textile weaving centers (handloom, putting-out system)

---

### B. COROMANDEL COAST (Tamil Country, Northern Circars)

**Centers**: Madras (Fort St. George, EIC), Pondicherry (French, about to be returned), Masulipatam, Nagapattinam

#### Trade Goods:

1. **Cotton textiles**:
   - **Madras region**: Chintzes (painted/printed cotton), calicos
   - **Northern Circars** (Andhra): Plain cotton cloth
   - Exported to Southeast Asia, Europe

2. **Diamonds**:
   - **Golconda** (near Hyderabad): Famous diamond mines (Kollur mines, declining by 1763 but still active)

3. **Tobacco**:
   - Coromandel tobacco plantations (local consumption + export)

**Economic Status 1763**: 
- **EIC-dominated trade**: Madras Presidency = EIC's textile export hub (southern India)
- **French collapse**: Pondicherry's trade stopped during Seven Years' War, awaiting restoration (minor 1763)

---

### C. GUJARAT

**Centers**: Surat (declining), Ahmedabad, Baroda, Cambay

#### Trade Goods:

1. **Cotton textiles**:
   - **Gujarat calicos**: Coarse cotton cloth, mass export to Africa, Middle East
   - **Ahmedabad**: Weaving center

2. **Opium** (Malwa):
   - **Malwa** (central India, bordering Gujarat): Opium production, exported via Gujarat ports (Cambay, later Bombay)
   - **1763**: Malwa opium = independent merchants, NOT EIC monopoly (vs Bengal opium)

3. **Indigo**:
   - Gujarat indigo (competing with Bengal)

**Economic Status 1763**: 
- **Maratha control**: Gaekwad (Baroda) controls Gujarat; taxation/trade under Maratha Sardars
- **Surat declining**: Once premier port (Mughal era), now secondary (EIC shifted to Bombay/Calcutta/Madras)

### D. MALABAR COAST (Kerala)

**Centers**: Calicut, Cochin, Trivandrum (Travancore), Tellicherry (EIC), Mahé (French)

#### Trade Goods:

1. **Pepper** (PRIMARY):
   - **Malabar pepper** (കുരുമുളക്): Black pepper, world's primary source; **Calicut, Cochin, Tellicherry** = export hubs
   - Grown in Western Ghats foothills (Kerala)

2. **Cardamom**:
   - **Malabar hills** (Kerala): Cardamom plantations

3. **Coconut/Coir**:
   - **Kerala coast**: Coconut fiber (coir) for ropes, exported

4. **Teak**:
   - **Malabar forests**: Teak timber (shipbuilding); EIC/Mysore/Travancore source

**Economic Status 1763**: 
- **Fragmented**: Small Kerala kingdoms (Cochin, Calicut, etc.) + Travancore (rising) + EIC/Dutch/French enclaves
- **Spice trade**: Europeans (EIC, Dutch, French) compete for pepper; **Dutch dominance fading** (VOC focus = East Indies)

### E. DECCAN (Hyderabad, Marathas, Mysore)

**Centers**: Hyderabad, Aurangabad, Pune, Seringapatam (Mysore)

   - **Deccan cotton**: Coarse cloth, internal trade

### F. CEYLON (SRI LANKA)

**Centers**: Colombo (Dutch), Galle (Dutch), Kandy (interior kingdom)

#### Trade Goods:

1. **Cinnamon** (PRIMARY):
   - **Ceylon cinnamon** (කරුවා): World's finest cinnamon (vs Cassia), **Dutch VOC monopoly** on coastal Ceylon
   - Grown in **southwestern lowlands** (Colombo, Galle hinterland)

2. **Areca nut** (betel nut):
   - Ceylon areca nut (පුවක්), internal + export trade

3. **Pearls**:
   - **Mannar coast** (northwest Ceylon): Pearl fisheries (Dutch-controlled)

**Economic Status 1763**: 
- **Dutch coastal strip**: VOC controls cinnamon trade (monopoly enforced militarily)
- **Kandy kingdom** (interior): Independent, **King Kirti Sri Rajasinha** (r. 1747-1782), resisting Dutch expansion

## VII. ECONOMY SUMMARY — Pre-Industrial Handicraft

**PRODUCTION**: 
- **Handloom textiles**: Bengal/Gujarat/Coromandel = mass export (millions of yards annually to Europe, Africa, Southeast Asia)
- **Spice monopolies**: Dutch = cinnamon (Ceylon), pepper trade fragmenting (EIC rising)
- **Shipbuilding**: Surat, Calcutta, Bombay, Malabar (teak) = major shipyards (EIC ships built in India)

**TRADE PATTERNS 1763**:
- **Europe ← India**: Textiles (50%+ EIC imports), saltpetre, indigo, silk, pepper
- **China ← India**: Opium (growing), cotton
- **Middle East/Africa ← India**: Coarse textiles, rice

**EUROPEAN ROLE 1763**: 
- **Merchants, NOT producers**: EIC/VOC = buy Indian goods via advances to weavers (*dadni* system), export
- **NOT YET de-industrialization**: Indian handicrafts = world-competitive; de-industrialization = 19th century (post-1815) when British machine textiles destroy handlooms

**DELTA vs 1815**: 
- **1763 = Indian manufacturing dominance**, Europeans = buyers
- **1815 = beginning de-industrialization**, British machine textiles starting to flood Indian markets (but Indian handicrafts still major)

## GAME DESIGN NOTES (For 1763 Re-Start)

**TERRITORIAL COMPLEXITY**: 1763 = vastly more fragmented than 1815:
- **No EIC territorial raj**: EIC = Bengal revenues + coastal forts only (Calcutta, Madras, Bombay); implement as Bengal vassal/protectorate or direct revenue control mechanic?
- **Maratha confederacy**: Needs multi-tag implementation (Peshwa + 4-5 great houses = semi-independent but confederacy bonds)
- **Mughal ghost**: Shah Alam II = powerless but symbolic (legitimacy/title mechanic? Can regional rulers seek Mughal sanction?)
- **Sikh misls**: 12 mobile confederacies, no fixed borders; tribal/raiding mechanics?

**ECONOMY**: 
- **Trade goods by province**: Implement Bengal muslin (Dhaka), pepper (Malabar), saltpetre (Bihar), cinnamon (Ceylon Dutch coast), diamonds (Golconda), etc.
- **Pre-industrial**: No factories; artisan pops (weavers, etc.); EIC = merchant extracting goods, not producer
- **Opium**: 1763 = small-scale; boom = post-1780s (tie to China trade events?)

- **Battle of Buxar (1764)**: EIC vs Awadh+Bengal+Shah Alam → EIC victory → Bengal *diwani* (1765)
- **Maratha resurgence (1770s)**: Madhavrao I's reconquest of North India
- **Anglo-Mysore Wars** (1767-99): Four wars, Tipu Sultan
- **Sikh rise**: Misls → Ranjit Singh's empire (1799-1801)
- **Maratha collapse**: Three Anglo-Maratha Wars (1775-1818)

**KEY DELTA vs 1815**: 
- **1763 = before EIC raj**; 1815 = after (massive shift)
- **1763 = Maratha/Mughal/successor-state multipolar**; 1815 = EIC hegemony
