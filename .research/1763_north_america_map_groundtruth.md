# 1763 North America — map ground-truth (for boot #15/#16/#17/#18)

Research digest (English encyclopedic sources; corroborate Louisiana footprint against Ekberg,

## 1. French/Spanish Louisiana — CONTROLLED footprint vs paper claim
At the 1763 partition Louisiana was a thin riverine corridor of ~12–15 posts, NOT the trans-plains interior.
- **Cession hinge:** Treaty of Fontainebleau 1762 (secret) → France cedes Louisiana WEST of the Mississippi +
  the Isle of Orleans (New Orleans) to **Spain**. Treaty of Paris Feb 1763 (public) → everything EAST of the
  Mississippi to **Britain**. Handover lagged (Fort de Chartres to Britain Oct 1765; Spain took New Orleans
  1766–69), but at the 1763 instant it's "French Louisiana being partitioned along the Mississippi".
- **Spanish Louisiana (= the LSA tag) controlled = WEST bank + New Orleans:** New Orleans, the lower-Mississippi
  west bank, **Ste. Genevieve** (the one significant west-side settlement, near St. Louis — St. Louis founded 1764),
  **Arkansas Post** (lower Arkansas R.), **Natchitoches** (Red River, western outpost facing Spanish Texas),
  lower-Missouri posts (Fort Orleans).
- **Went to BRITAIN (east bank — NOT LSA):** the Illinois Country villages Kaskaskia / Cahokia / Prairie du Rocher /
  Fort de Chartres / St. Philippe; Fort Vincennes (Wabash); Natchez/Fort Rosalie; Mobile & Biloxi (Gulf coast).
- **Paper claim only (→ should be Native, item #16):** the western Great Plains, Missouri headwaters, Rockies watershed.

## 2. Native polities c.1763 (coherent peoples, mostly decentralized towns/bands)
- **Southeast (inland behind the coastal colonies):** Cherokee (SW NC/SE TN/N GA/W SC/NE AL/SW VA), Muscogee/Creek
  (GA/AL/N FL), Choctaw (MS/LA/AL), Chickasaw (NE MS/NW AL/W TN/SW KY), Catawba (SC/NC piedmont — a UNIFIED small nation).
- **Iroquois / Haudenosaunee** — upstate NY core into the Ohio Country; the MOST centralized (Six Nations Grand Council).
- **Great Lakes & Ohio (Pontiac's War 1763, no unison):** Odawa/Ottawa, Ojibwe, Potawatomi, Wyandot/Huron, Miami/Wea,
  Shawnee, Delaware/Lenape, Mingo, Seneca, Kickapoo/Mascouten/Piankashaw. **Illinois Confederation** (5 tribes, a Great Chief — relatively unified).
- **Plains / interior west (the paper-claim zone → Native):** Comanche (Comanchería: NW TX/E NM/SE CO/SW KS/W OK),
  Osage (MO/AR/KS/OK), Sioux (Dakota east MN/WI; Lakota expanding west onto the N plains), Pawnee (Platte/Loup, NE),
  Blackfoot (N plains MT/AB), Wabanaki (NE Maine/Maritimes — confirm separately).

## 3. Spanish-America disruption timeline 1789–1815 (N America / Gulf)
- 1 Oct 1800 — Treaty of San Ildefonso: Spain retrocedes Louisiana to Napoleonic France.
- 30 Apr 1803 — Louisiana Purchase (transfer 20 Dec 1803); Spain administered it until 1803.
- 5–6 May 1808 — Abdications of Bayonne (Charles IV, Ferdinand VII); Joseph Bonaparte installed → colonial juntas.
- 16 Sept 1810 — Grito de Dolores (Hidalgo) launches the Mexican War of Independence.
- 23–26 Sept 1810 — Republic of West Florida (rebels seize Baton Rouge).
- 27 Oct 1810 — Madison proclaims US annexation of West Florida; occupation done 6–10 Dec 1810.
- 1811 Hidalgo executed; 1813 Mexican insurgents declare independence.

## 4. The 13 Colonies / British seaboard 1763
- Connected Atlantic coastal strip east of the Appalachian crest. SC (Charleston) & GA (Savannah, f.1733) firmly
  British; Georgia the southernmost (bordering Spanish/then-British Florida).
- Royal Proclamation of 7 Oct 1763: bars settlement WEST of the Appalachian watershed (Indian Reserve). Crown's
  rule of thumb: Atlantic-draining rivers = colonial; Mississippi-draining = Native reserve.
- **Map guidance:** seaboard = connected British strip down through Georgia (boot #14 restored Charleston/Savannah);
  trans-Appalachian west = Native, not colonial (boot #16).
