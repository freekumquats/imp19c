# Research: Oceania (Australia / New Guinea / New Zealand) circa 1763 (BT-K)
Source: background research agent 2026-07-19, Wikipedia-synthesized. Reference only.
1763 = PRE-European-contact for AU/NZ (Cook's 1st voyage 1768-71).

## Australia (~500k-750k people) — model as CULTURES (non-state), not tags
Cultures by zone: (1) Pama-Nyungan SE/coastal (Eora, Kulin/Wurundjeri, Wiradjuri);
(2) Pama-Nyungan interior/desert (Arrernte); (3) Pama-Nyungan SW (Noongar);
(4) non-Pama-Nyungan north (Yolŋu, Arnhem/Kimberley); (5) Tasmanian (Palawa, isolated).
Density highest QLD/NSW/coast/Murray. PRIORITY HIGH.

## New Guinea (millions, highlands dense) — model as CULTURES, non-state
(1) Trans-NG Highland (Dani/Huli/Enga/Chimbu); (2) Sepik lowland; (3) S Papuan lowland;
(4) coastal Austronesian-Papuan; (5) West Papuan (Bird's Head). No states 1763. PRIORITY MEDIUM.

## New Zealand (~110k) — ORGANIZED: one Māori culture + iwi TAGS
North I.: Ngāpuhi(Northland), Ngāti Porou(East Coast), Ngāti Kahungunu(Hawke's Bay),
Waikato/Tainui, Ngāti Tūwharetoa(central), Tūhoe, Ngāti Maniapoto, Te Atiawa(Taranaki).
South I.: Ngāi Tahu (dominant, most of island), Kāti Māmoe + Waitaha (absorbed/vassal).
Pre-Musket-Wars. PRIORITY HIGH.

## Nearby islands
Fiji: hierarchical chiefdoms (Bau/Rewa/Kai Colo) — Fijian culture, ~100k+. HIGH.
Tonga: Tuʻi Kanokupolu dynasty dominant by 1760s — Tongan culture, single tag. HIGH.
Samoa: Fa'amatai/tafa'ifa system — Samoan culture. HIGH.
Vanuatu (100+ langs, fragmented), New Caledonia (Kanak), Solomons — Melanesian cultures. MED/LOW.
